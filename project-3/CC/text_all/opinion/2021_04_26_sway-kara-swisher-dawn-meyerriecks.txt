The C.I.A.’s Top Technologist Is Uncomfortable With Facebook
The Central Intelligence Agency poaches puppeteers from Hollywood and partners with big tech firms from Silicon Valley. It has to, says the agency’s top technologist, Dawn Meyerriecks, if it wants cutting-edge drones, hidden cameras, and deep cover that can bypass biometric scansMeyerriecks oversees spy gear as the head of the C.I.A.’s Directorate of Science and Technology. (Think “Q Branch” from the James Bond films.) But the nimble, discreet monitoring advanced by the intelligence community also raises big questions for the privacy and data security of American citizens. (Now, think about what Edward Snowden revealed about the N.S.A.).
