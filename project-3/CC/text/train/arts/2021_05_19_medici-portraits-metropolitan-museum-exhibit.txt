Talking Across the Centuries to the Medici Family
This article is part of our latest special report on Museums, which focuses on reopening, reinvention and resilience.The famous and powerful family that once controlled Florence provides the big name in “The Medici: Portraits and Politics, 1512-1570,” a large exhibition about to open at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York.But sorry, Lorenzo, Giovanni and Catherine — the exhibition is not really about you, even if you were the best-known members of the dynasty that held sway for 300 years and inspired one of the most extraordinary periods in history, the Renaissance.
